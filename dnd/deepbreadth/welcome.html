Welcome to Deep Breadth

Prepare to walk the streets of a modern world filled with technology while still wielding wondrous magic powers.

Welcome to Cogitar (COH-jih-tahr), the Heart of Hiraeth (HEER-yth). This urban metropolis is a testament to humans and their growing ability, but that is how the Shadewalkers saw the world. Lightbearers knew the truth; fantastic beings began blending with this world, and only they could see the magic woven into the land. Advancements in technology, from the light switch that brings light to our houses to the intricate network in our mobile telecommunication devices, are all manifestations of powerful magic users.

When the Veil Tore Open

It has been quite some time. Many still remember the day the boundary tore and the magic hidden among us was revealed. Humans obscured in the shade were forced to deal with a world larger than themselves all around them, while the beings bathed in the light had no choice but to collaborate with the magically deficient. Realms shattered, sending hundreds of thousands of beings into this one and sealing it away. Urgency made it important to work together as the fantastical creatures merged with the known world, as did their monsters and the following destruction. Creatures terrifying might sunder the land and eliminate much of society, leaving this as the last known bastion of civilization. Ignorance truly was bliss. We departed from the Ignolorian (ihg-NOH-lohr-ee-an) Era, the age of unknown horizons, and into what many consider the Melugium (Meh-LOO-zhee-uhm) Era, the age of restored balance.

Where we Now Stand

From the outside of the city, it is a paradise for society. Lands have been torn apart by the monsters that roamed the land without substantial forces to drive them away. Death and destruction worldwide remain a distance away from the great metropolis. Corogus (coh-ROH-guhs) sits center as the downtown district where all major business takes place and is the only access to the government district of Aeorza (a-YOR-zuh) to the east across a lone bridge suspended above the massive river. Grimhaven is the core of industrialism and factories north of the center and opposite Rheinshire (RINE-shi-er) to the south, where it remains expensive but worthy of staying. Those lucky enough to be closer to the center find their greatest concern being traffic, although nothing stops criminals from trying their hand at fortune. However, people living west of the city center in Orion Heights are left with less than suitable living conditions. This is particularly true the further out one finds themselves of Grimhaven and Rheinshire. Safety exists as a wall at the edge, a boundary, and a symbol for what remains.
